This page is built for operators and tower‑crane climbers who actually live in the seat and on the steel: frozen tracks, mud, wind, and long climbs. Everything here is about keeping the machine moving and the operator warm, safe, and sharp.

Ground tools for tracks and mud

Digging & clearing

Spade shovels

  • Long‑handle spade for cutting out frozen tracks
  • Square shovel for cleanup around pads and mats
  • Heavy‑duty steel, not box‑store flex

Use for digging out idlers, rollers,and cleaning around sprockets when everything’s packed with frozen fill.

Track maintenance

Track bars & ice tools

  • Track bar for rolling chain and lining pads
  • Ice chisel / digging bar for frozen buildup
  • Scrapers for cleaning steps and platforms

Keeps the machine moving when freeze‑thaw turns everything into concrete.

Support

Buckets, rigging & storage

  • Heavy buckets for tools and chains
  • Rigging storage: slings, chokers, shackles
  • Dedicated “operator kit” that lives in the cab

Operator shouldn’t be hunting tools—everything staged and labeled.

Cold‑weather & climb gear

Hands

Operator gloves

  • Insulated but still thin enough for controls
  • Wind‑resistant backs, grippy palms
  • Backup pair in the cab at all times

Different from laborer gloves—built for levers, joysticks, and long sits.

Climbers

Tower crane & ladder climbs

  • Climbing harness rated for tower access (where required)
  • Lightweight, layered clothing for long climbs
  • Helmet with chin strap and clear/tinted lenses

Built for the guys climbing tower cranes and high‑rise rigs in wind and ice.

Warmth

Base layers & hoodies

  • Moisture‑wicking base layer (no cotton against skin)
  • Branded hoodie over insulated vest or jacket
  • Neck gaiter / balaclava for cab doors that never seal right

This is where your branded gear lives—operators wearing your logo all winter.

Cab kit & consumables

Cab essentials

Operator cab kit

  • Markers, paint sticks, and layout chalk
  • Notepad, job info, lift charts, emergency contacts
  • Small first‑aid and eye‑wash bottles

Everything staged so the operator can answer questions without leaving the seat.

Fasteners

Tapcons, drill bits & anchors

  • Concrete bits matched to Tapcon sizes
  • Anchor kits for temporary brackets and supports
  • Labeled boxes stored in a dedicated bin

These also show up on your Partners and Consumables pages for affiliate links.
