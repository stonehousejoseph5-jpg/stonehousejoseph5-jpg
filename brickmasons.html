The Brick Mason Trade

Brick masons are precision workers — every line, every joint, every course has to be perfect. Whether it’s block, brick, stone, veneer, or structural walls, masons build the surfaces people see first and judge the hardest. Their tools are simple, but the skill behind them is earned through years of real jobsite experience.

This section covers the tools brick masons rely on daily: trowels, levels, line tools, cutting tools, and everything needed to lay clean, straight, tight work that lasts for generations.

Trowels & Hand Tools

Precision

Brick Trowels

  • London or Philadelphia pattern
  • Used for spreading and shaping mortar
  • Essential for every mason on every job

The main tool of the trade — never leave home without it.

Detail

Tuck Pointer

  • For packing mortar into tight joints
  • Used for repair and finish work
  • Great for brick restoration

Perfect for tight spaces and clean joint work.

Finishing

Jointers

  • Concave, V‑joint, and grapevine styles
  • Used to finish mortar joints
  • Creates clean, consistent lines

The final touch that makes a wall look professional.

Shaping

Cold & Bolster Chisels

  • Cutting brick and block
  • Shaping edges and corners
  • Used with a mason’s hammer

For when the saw is too much and precision matters.

General Use

Mason’s Hammer

  • Cutting brick
  • Chipping mortar
  • General striking tool

Half hammer, half chisel — all mason.

Smoothing

Rub Brick

  • Smooths rough surfaces
  • Cleans high spots
  • Used for block and concrete

Makes walls look clean and uniform.

Levels & Line Tools

Accuracy

4‑Foot & 2‑Foot Levels

  • Ensures plumb and level courses
  • Aluminum or box‑beam construction
  • Jobsite‑tough and reliable

A mason’s wall is only as good as the level behind it.

Layout

Mason’s Line

  • Nylon line for straight courses
  • Used with line blocks or pins
  • High‑visibility colors

Keeps every course straight from end to end.

Setup

Line Blocks & Line Pins

  • Holds mason’s line in place
  • Used on corners and leads
  • Essential for layout

Simple tools that make perfect walls possible.

Cutting & Mixing Tools

Cutting

Brick Saws

  • Wet saws for clean cuts
  • Diamond blades for brick and block
  • Used for precision work

Keeps cuts clean and reduces chipping.

Mixing

Mortar Mixer

  • Mixes mortar consistently
  • Gas or electric options
  • Used for large jobs

Keeps the mud flowing so the wall keeps growing.

Hand Mixing

Mixing Tubs & Hoes

  • Small‑batch mortar mixing
  • Easy to move around the site
  • Perfect for repairs

For when you don’t need a full mixer running.

Safety Gear

Protection

Gloves

  • Cut‑resistant
  • Impact‑resistant
  • Mortar‑resistant

Mortar eats skin — gloves save hands.

Eyes

Safety Glasses

  • Impact‑rated
  • Dust protection
  • Clear or tinted

Brick chips fly — protect your eyes.

Respiratory

Dust Masks

  • Filters brick and mortar dust
  • Lightweight and breathable
  • Disposable or reusable

Mortar dust is no joke — protect your lungs.
