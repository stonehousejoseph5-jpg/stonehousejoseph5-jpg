About the Carpenter Trade

Carpenters are one of the core trades that hold a jobsite together. From the first layout lines snapped on bare concrete to the final finish details that bring a building to life, carpenters touch every phase of construction. They frame the structure, build the forms, set the layout, hang the board, install the ceilings, and handle the precision work that keeps every other trade moving forward.

On a real jobsite nothing happens alone.Carpenters work side by side with laborers,ironworkers, operators,electricians,plumbers, finishers, and every craft on the project. Each trade brings their skill,but carpenters are often the ones setting the pace—building the paths,the platforms,the forms,the walls, and the systems that everyone else relies on. It’s a team effort from start to finish, and carpenters are right in the middle of it.

This page is built to reflect the full scope of the trade. Not just the basic tools — but the real gear used by union carpenters on commercial, industrial, and heavy‑civil projects.Layout tools,framing tools,drywall tools, metal stud tools, power tools, safety gear, jobsite essentials, and everything in between. If it’s listed here,it’s because it’s used in the field every day by the people who build the work.
