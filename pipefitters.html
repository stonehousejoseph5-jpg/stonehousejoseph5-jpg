About the Pipefitter Trade

Pipefitters are the backbone of industrial construction.Power plants,refineries, factories,hospitals,data centers—none of it runs without the miles of pipe these crews install, weld, support, and pressure‑test.From high‑pressure steam lines to chilled water, gas, chemical, and process piping, pipefitters work with systems that keep entire facilities alive.

On the biggest jobs in the country — places with a hundred cranes swinging at once and pipe racks stretching as far as you can see—it’s the union pipefitters who make it happen. They work side by side with welders,operators,ironworkers,laborers, electricians, and every other craft on the site. Every spool, every flange, every weld, every hanger, every valve — it all has to be right. There’s no guessing when you’re dealing with pressure, heat, and industrial systems.

This page highlights the tools, gear, and equipment pipefitters rely on every day. From layout and prep to cutting, threading, rigging, and installation, these tools reflect the real work done on commercial and industrial jobsites across the country. Whether you're fitting stainless, carbon, copper, PVC, or specialty pipe, this list covers the essentials used by the crews who build and maintain the systems that keep the world running.

Pipefitters are problem‑solvers, precision workers, and heavy‑industrial craftsmen. This page honors their skill,their union pride, and the work they do on the biggest and toughest projects out there.
