Safety Gear – Introduction

Safety gear isn’t optional on a real jobsite-it’s the difference between going home at the end of the day or not.Every trade relies on equipment that protects workers from falls, impacts, workers from falls, impacts,chemical exposure, heavy lifts, and the unexpected emergencies that happen more often than people admit. From basic PPE like safety glasses and gloves to high‑visibility shirts and vests, every item has a purpose and a standard it must meet.

Rigging and lifting gear is especially critical. Straps and slings are taken out of service the moment any of the internal color-fibers show — no exceptions. Whether it’s a 10‑foot or 20‑foot strap, 3‑inch wide for heavy equipment lifts, or smaller slings for lighter loads, damaged rigging is never used. These straps are rated to pick up skid steers, Bobcats, and other heavy items, and they’re inspected constantly because one failure can cost a life.

Jobsite safety also includes the things people don’t think about until they’re needed: first‑aid kits, eyewash stations, AEDs, and overdose‑ response supplies like Narcan. Emergencies happen on construction sites —from falls to heat stress to overdoses — and crews need to be prepared. High‑visibility clothing, caution tape, danger tape, and flagging help keep workers seen and hazards marked.

This section covers the essential safety gear used across trades, the purpose of each item, and the standards that keep workers protected.
